The order of work

I have been working in the field of web development and website promotion since 2001, and I have been working with 1C-Bitrix products since 2006.

Main stages of work

1. You leave a request for the development of the site (on the "Contacts" page)
2. I send you a brief to fill out, we agree on a meeting to discuss the project;
3. I study the brief, draw up an estimate and schedule of work (in some cases it is required to write a full-fledged Terms of Reference), which we are discussing with you;
4. After agreeing on the cost and terms of work, I prepare a contract, we sign it (if necessary);
5. In case you need to select a design template, I select the design options (templates) according to your wishes, you review them and select a template for your site; 6. Next, I am doing the selection, purchase, installation and configuration of the platform "Bitrix";
7. I perform the design integration;
8. I create pages, content and do SEO settings;
9. I create a directory structure, upload data to the site database;
10. I perform the settings for catalog displaying and, if necessary, additional programming;
11. I perform synchronization with 1C: configuration, debugging, testing;
12. I spend all other necessary work on the content;
13. I place the site on a hosting, I carry out necessary settings;
14. I fulfill all points of the "Quality Monitor" PU Bitrix and testing of the project as a whole;
15. We sign the Work Completion Certificate, you make the payment, after which I transfer you all the necessary data to access the hosting and site control panel;
16. If necessary, users are trained to work with the site;

17. After completion of the site creation, I can take your site in support.

Payment is possible in stages, in accordance with the work schedule specified in the Contract.

Why does it make sense to work with a private webmaster?

By placing your future website in the hands of a private webmaster, you first of all avoid communicating with intermediaries (usually managers), and have the opportunity convey your ideas to the developer faster and more clearly. This reduces the number of errors and moments of misunderstanding and, accordingly, the time for website development.

As a rule, working with a private webmaster is more profitable from a financial point of view, since the cost of an hour for a specialist in the company is much higher (usually 1.5-2 times).
